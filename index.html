Why?

Nine billion mouths will have to be fed daily by 2050, but food crisis is already an evidence in underdeveloped countries all around the globe. In the meantime food production technology and the intelligence around it also evolve perpetuating and empowering these asymmetries.

Open Agriculture can play a very important role and integrate possible solutions for these problems. However, all the evidences indicate that most farmers are far from adopting high-end technological solutions and when they do, they are even more far from understanding the technology itself, in a sense that would confer autonomy, decision and proprietorship to the same adoption process. The opposite is much more evident as AgTech companies tend to keep under their control, own will and sometimes self-interest, important components of the solution they preconize, such as data sets, exclusivity over software usage, and sometimes even firmware. It is our firm belief that the existence and commons management of a set of resources aimed at technology development in an open environment, can lead to the adoption of Open Agriculture Technology and Practices.

In this sense, the path for the formation of a Farm Labs Network can be initiated through the design and development of a common repository aimed at the identification, classification development of Open Agriculture elements, such as technologies, best practices, data sets, seeds, and other open or public domain knowledge.

If you are an enthusiast about the topic of Open Agriculture, there is an immense treasure trove of openly licensed information about hardware, software, best practices and community activities. However this information is scattered, mostly non-interoperable and sometimes of dubious licensing status.

Farmlabs.cc aims to be the main repository of knowledge for the Farmlabs network, a community of local open agriculture labs, aimed at sharing, testing and developing that very same knowledge.

Farmlabs.cc should become an open platform to:

  • collect meta-data about all the existing Open Agriculture or related projects;
  • catalog them into part repositories for meta-projects;
  • establish clear information about their licensing status;
  • mark areas that are good for community involvement in upstream projects.

In this two-day Dev Sprint, teams of designers, developers and stakeholders will kickstart the community development of this platform.
